Sunflowers

The quintessential summer flower. Sunflowers are fast-growing, cheerful, and generous — with seeds for the birds at the end of the season.

Full sun, 6–8 hrs

Growing Basics

  • Sunflowers are annuals in Minnesota — frost-tender but fast-growing once the soil warms up.
  • Sunlight: Full sun (6–8 hours daily). Sunflowers track the sun when young, so try to face them east or south.
  • Soil: Well-drained, average to fertile soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH.
  • Transplanting: Plant after your last frost date, once soil has warmed. Sunflowers have taproots, so these plants were grown directly in their sale pots — plant the whole root ball without disturbing the roots. Dig a generously deep hole and set them in carefully.
  • Watering: Water deeply once or twice a week rather than frequent shallow watering. Deep watering encourages deep roots, which makes plants more stable and drought-tolerant. Once established, sunflowers are quite tough.
  • Fertilization: A balanced fertilizer at planting time helps. Avoid overfeeding with nitrogen — it promotes leafy growth at the expense of flowers.

Other Tips

  • Tall varieties (5–10 feet) will need staking in exposed or windy spots, especially after they develop a heavy flower head.
  • Leave seed heads standing at the end of the season — goldfinches and chickadees go wild for them.
  • You can also harvest the seeds yourself: wait until the back of the head turns brown, then cut and hang upside down to dry. Toast the seeds with salt for a great snack.
  • Squirrels love sunflower seeds too — if they're a problem, a hardware cloth cage around the head protects the harvest.
