Sage

Soft, silvery leaves with a savory, earthy flavor. Great for poultry, stuffing, and herbal tea blends.

Growing Basics

  • Sage is a perennial in Minnesota, but may need winter protection in pots.
  • Sunlight: Full sun (6+ hours daily).
  • Soil: Well-drained, sandy or loamy soil.
  • Transplanting: Plant gently at the same depth as in the pot.
  • Watering: Let soil dry between waterings; drought-tolerant once established.
  • Fertilization: Fertilize every 6-8 weeks with compost or a low-nitrogen fertilizer (5-10-10).

Other Tips

  • Prune to prevent woodiness and encourage new growth.
