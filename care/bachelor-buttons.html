Bachelor's Buttons

Also called cornflower, these vivid blue (and sometimes pink or white) blooms are cottage garden classics. They're tough, cold-tolerant, and beloved by pollinators.

Growing Basics

  • Bachelor's buttons are cool-season annuals in Minnesota. They bloom in spring and early summer, then slow down in peak heat.
  • Sunlight: Full sun (6+ hours daily).
  • Soil: Average, well-drained soil. They don't need rich conditions — too much fertility leads to floppy plants.
  • Transplanting: Cold-tolerant — can go in the ground a few weeks before your last frost date. Handle roots gently at planting time.
  • Watering: Water regularly until established. Once growing, they're fairly drought-tolerant. Avoid overwatering — soggy soil is their main enemy.
  • Fertilization: A light application at planting is all they need. Skip the heavy feeding.

Taproot Note — Important!

  • Bachelor's buttons have a taproot and don't like having their roots disturbed. These plants were grown directly in their sale pots — just plant the whole root ball as-is. No need to separate or loosen the roots.
  • Dig a hole, slip the whole thing in, and backfill gently. They'll take off from there.

Other Tips

  • Deadhead to encourage more blooms, but leave a few spent flowers at the end of the season if you want them to self-seed next year.
  • Taller varieties (18–30") may benefit from staking in windy spots.
  • Excellent cut flower — blooms last well in a vase.
  • A magnet for bees and butterflies.
