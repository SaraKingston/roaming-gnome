Summer Squash

Including green zucchini and yellow squash. Prolific growers with mild flavor and tender skin, great for grilling, baking, or stir-fry.

Growing Basics

  • Squash are annuals in Minnesota.
  • Sunlight: Full sun (6+ hours daily).
  • Soil: Well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
  • Transplanting: Plant at the same depth as in the pot when soil reaches 65°; handle gently to avoid disturbing roots. These guys don't like to share space! If your seedling container has multiple plants, cut all but 1 down to soil level (don't pull them out, you don't want to disturb the roots); otherwise, space them about 12" apart.
  • Watering: Keep soil consistently moist; water at the base to avoid mildew.
  • Fertilization: Use balanced fertilizer (10-10-10) every 2-3 weeks; increase potassium during fruiting.

Other Tips

  • Mulch to retain moisture.
  • Harvest when fruits are 6-8 inches long for best flavor and texture. Pick often to encourage more fruit.
